Biochemical Effects of Ischemia on Cardiac Metabolism

1. Normal Cardiac Metabolism (Aerobic State)

The heart primarily oxidizes fatty acids (~70%) and glucose (~30%) for ATP production.

Fatty Acid Oxidation

  • Fatty acids enter cells via FAT/CD36 transporters.
  • β-oxidation generates Acetyl-CoA, NADH, and FADH₂.
  • ATP Yield: ~106 ATP per palmitate (high but oxygen-intensive).

Glucose Oxidation

  • Glucose enters via GLUT-4, converted to pyruvate through glycolysis.
  • Pyruvate enters the TCA cycle via pyruvate dehydrogenase (PDH).
  • ATP Yield: ~32 ATP per glucose (lower but oxygen-efficient).

2. Metabolic Shifts During Ischemia

Ischemia reduces oxygen supply, forcing the heart to switch to anaerobic glycolysis, which is inefficient.

Key Effects:

  • β-Oxidation slows down → Fatty acid metabolism inhibited.
  • Glucose metabolism shifts to anaerobic glycolysis.
  • 2 ATP per glucose → Energy crisis.
  • Lactate accumulation → Lactic acidosis.

3. Reperfusion Injury

After oxygen restoration, excessive fatty acid oxidation and ROS formation lead to more damage.

Effects:

  • Sudden oxygen influx → ROS generation.
  • Glucose oxidation still suppressed.
  • Mitochondrial damage → Arrhythmias & Cell Death.
